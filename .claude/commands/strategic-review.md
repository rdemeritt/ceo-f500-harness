# /strategic-review — Annual Strategic Plan Review

## Purpose

Facilitates the annual strategic planning process. Reviews performance against current strategy, identifies strategic shifts, and produces the next-year strategic plan for Board approval.

## Usage

```
/strategic-review [YEAR for planning]
```

---

## Strategic Review Timeline

| Phase | Timing | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| External environment scan | 12 weeks before fiscal year-end | Chief of Staff + BU Heads |
| Competitive analysis | 10 weeks before FYE | CMO + CRO |
| Internal performance review | 8 weeks before FYE | All C-suite |
| Strategic options workshop | 6 weeks before FYE | CEO-led |
| Resource allocation | 5 weeks before FYE | CEO + CFO + COO |
| Draft strategic plan | 4 weeks before FYE | Chief of Staff |
| Executive alignment | 3 weeks before FYE | CEO + all C-suite |
| Board review and approval | 2 weeks before FYE | Board |
| Cascade to organization | Week 1 of new fiscal year | All C-suite |

## Strategic Review Structure

### Part 1: Where We've Been
- Performance vs. last year's strategic plan
- OKR achievement rate (company level)
- Financial performance vs. plan
- Key strategic wins and misses
- Market position change (gained/lost)

### Part 2: Where We Are Now
- Current competitive position (market share, differentiation)
- Core strengths (what we do better than anyone)
- Core weaknesses (honest — where we're behind)
- Technology position
- Talent position
- Financial strength and constraints

### Part 3: Where the Market Is Going
*CMO + CRO + CTO lead*
- Market size and growth trajectory (next 3 years)
- Customer need evolution
- Competitive dynamics (who's gaining, who's losing)
- Technology disruption threats
- Regulatory landscape changes
- Macro trends affecting the business

### Part 4: Strategic Options
*CEO facilitates — all executives contribute*

For each strategic option:
- Opportunity description
- Financial potential (revenue impact, investment required)
- Execution risk
- Time to value
- Strategic fit score
- Resource requirements

### Part 5: Strategic Choices
*CEO decides — Board approves*

Define the 3-year strategic direction:
- Where to play (markets, customers, geographies)
- How to win (differentiation, value proposition)
- What to build vs. buy vs. partner
- What to stop doing (critical — scarcity is strategic)
- Resource allocation priority order

### Part 6: OKRs for Coming Year
*CEO sets company OKRs — C-suite sets department OKRs*

Company OKRs (3–5 maximum):
```
Objective: [Inspiring, qualitative direction]
  KR1: [Specific, measurable, time-bound]
  KR2: [Specific, measurable, time-bound]
  KR3: [Specific, measurable, time-bound]
```

### Part 7: Resource Plan
*CFO + COO lead*
- Budget allocation by strategic priority
- Headcount plan by function
- Capex investment schedule
- Initiative roadmap for the year

---

## Board Presentation Format

- 20–25 slide limit
- Executive summary on slide 1
- One slide per major strategic choice
- Clear "what we're saying no to" slide (critical for discipline)
- Financial projections with assumptions
- Risk-adjusted scenarios (base, upside, downside)
