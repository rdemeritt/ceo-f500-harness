# Skill: Financial Reporting

## Purpose
Patterns for producing accurate, compliant financial reports — P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, board packages, and investor communications.

## When to Invoke
- Producing any financial statement or management report
- Preparing Board financial package
- Building financial models or forecasts
- Analyzing financial performance vs. plan

## Core Patterns

### P&L Report Structure
```
Revenue
  Gross Revenue
  (-) Returns and Allowances
  = Net Revenue

Cost of Revenue
  Direct Labor
  Direct Materials
  Other COGS
  = Total Cost of Revenue

= Gross Profit
  Gross Margin %

Operating Expenses
  Sales and Marketing
  Research and Development
  General and Administrative
  = Total OpEx

= EBITDA
  Depreciation and Amortization
= EBIT (Operating Income)
  Interest Expense / Income
  Other Income / Expense
= Pre-tax Income
  Income Tax Provision
= Net Income
  EPS (diluted)
```

### Variance Reporting Rule
Always explain variances >5% from plan or prior period:
- What drove the variance
- Is it timing (catch-up expected) or structural (permanent shift)?
- What's the outlook effect?

### Non-GAAP Metrics
If using non-GAAP metrics (Adjusted EBITDA, etc.):
- Always provide GAAP reconciliation
- Justify why non-GAAP is relevant
- Never present non-GAAP more prominently than GAAP (GC + CFO rule)

## Compliance Notes
- Public companies: follow SEC Regulation S-X and S-K
- Revenue recognition: ASC 606 (US GAAP) or IFRS 15
- All estimates and assumptions must be documented
