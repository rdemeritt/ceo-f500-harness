# Skill: Legal Review

## Purpose
Patterns for conducting legal reviews, structuring contracts, identifying legal risk, and producing legal opinions.

## When to Invoke
- Reviewing contracts or agreements
- Assessing legal risk in a decision
- Responding to regulatory inquiry
- Preparing legal opinion or memo

## Contract Review Checklist

### Core Provisions to Review
1. **Parties** — Correct legal entities? Authorized signatories?
2. **Term and termination** — Duration, renewal, termination rights, notice periods
3. **Payment terms** — Amount, timing, late fees, dispute process
4. **Scope of work** — Specific, measurable, no ambiguity
5. **IP ownership** — Who owns what is created? License back?
6. **Liability cap** — Is it reasonable? Carved-out exceptions?
7. **Indemnification** — What are we indemnifying? Is it mutual?
8. **Warranty** — What are we warranting? Can we deliver?
9. **Governing law and jurisdiction** — Favorable? Practical?
10. **Data privacy** — GDPR/CCPA compliant? DPA needed?
11. **Confidentiality** — Adequate protection for our information?
12. **Force majeure** — Adequate? Covers pandemic/cyber?
13. **Change of control** — Assignment rights on acquisition?

### Risk Rating by Provision
| Provision Risk | Score |
|---|---|
| Unlimited liability | 10 — reject or heavily negotiate |
| One-sided termination | 8 — negotiate mutual rights |
| Weak IP protection | 7 — critical for core business IP |
| Missing data privacy | 8 — regulatory exposure |
| Short limitation period | 5 — context-dependent |
| Standard limitation | 2 — normal |

## Legal Memo Structure
```
LEGAL MEMORANDUM
To: [Requestor]
From: General Counsel
Date: [ISO date]
Re: [Matter]
Classification: ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED

QUESTION PRESENTED:
[Specific legal question]

SHORT ANSWER:
[One paragraph conclusion]

ANALYSIS:
[Full legal analysis with citations]

APPLICABLE LAW:
[Statutes, regulations, case law]

CONCLUSION:
[Recommendation]

LIMITATIONS:
[Any caveats on this opinion]
```

## Privilege Protection
Mark all GC communications during crisis or dispute:
`CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED — DO NOT FORWARD`
