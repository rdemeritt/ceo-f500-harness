# Skill: Investor Relations

## Purpose
Patterns for investor communications, earnings calls, roadshows, and managing the analyst/investor relationship.

## When to Invoke
- Preparing earnings materials
- Drafting investor presentations
- Responding to investor inquiries
- Planning investor events

## Reg FD Compliance (Public Companies)
**Always active — GC enforces.**
- No selective disclosure of material non-public information
- All material disclosures must be simultaneous and public
- Analyst meetings may only cover information already public
- New material information disclosed in analyst meeting → immediate public disclosure required (8-K or press release)

## Earnings Call Script Pattern

### CEO Opening (5–7 minutes)
```
1. Thank operator and welcome participants
2. Reference to press release and 10-Q/10-K filed
3. Safe harbor statement (read verbatim — GC provides)
4. Quarter narrative: what we accomplished, key wins
5. Strategic progress: where we are vs. our plan
6. Look forward: 1 or 2 forward-looking statements (approved by GC + CFO)
7. Hand off to CFO
```

### CFO Walk (8–10 minutes)
```
1. Revenue: total + by segment; growth rate; vs. consensus
2. Gross margin: rate + direction + key drivers
3. OpEx: by line item; leverage or investment story
4. EBITDA/Operating income: rate + $ + vs. consensus
5. EPS: diluted; vs. consensus
6. Cash: end of period; free cash flow; uses
7. Balance sheet highlights (debt, inventory, DSO as relevant)
8. Guidance (if providing): current quarter and/or full year
9. Key assumptions in guidance
10. Hand back to CEO for Q&A
```

## Q&A Handling Rules
- Answer the question asked — don't pivot to a different topic
- If you don't know: "I'll follow up with you after the call"
- If you can't discuss (legal, regulatory): "We can't comment on that at this time"
- If you disagree with the premise: say so, politely, with data
- Never get defensive — it signals something is wrong

## Investor Presentation Structure (Non-Earnings)
1. Company overview (what we do, market size, customers)
2. Problem we solve (why the market needs us)
3. Our solution (product/service differentiation)
4. Business model (how we make money, unit economics)
5. Market opportunity (TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology)
6. Go-to-market (how we reach and retain customers)
7. Competitive landscape (honest — investors know the space)
8. Financial performance (actuals + projections with assumptions)
9. Management team (relevant experience)
10. Use of proceeds (if raising capital)
