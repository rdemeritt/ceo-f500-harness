# Skill: Corporate Communications

## Purpose
Patterns for executive communications, press releases, crisis statements, investor communications, and internal announcements.

## When to Invoke
- Drafting any external-facing communication
- Preparing CEO or executive remarks
- Writing press releases or statements
- Internal company-wide communications

## GC Review Requirement
**Every external communication requires GC review before release — no exceptions.**

## Communication Patterns

### Press Release Structure
```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE [or EMBARGOED UNTIL: date/time]

[COMPANY NAME] [ACTION VERB] [KEY NEWS]

[CITY, Date] — [Company Name] (Ticker: XXX), [one-line company description], today announced [key news in 1–2 sentences].

[CONTEXT PARAGRAPH: Why this matters, market context]

[QUOTE from CEO — 2–3 sentences, forward-looking, on-brand]

[DETAIL PARAGRAPHS: Supporting information, data, how it works]

[QUOTE from other executive if relevant]

[FORWARD-LOOKING STATEMENT DISCLAIMER — Required. GC provides language.]

About [Company Name]:
[2–3 sentence boilerplate. Keep current — CFO and GC approve any changes.]

Contacts:
Media: [Name, email, phone]
Investors: [Name, email, phone]
```

### CEO Communication Principles
1. **Lead with the headline** — don't bury the news
2. **Be specific** — numbers, dates, names (not "significant" or "soon")
3. **Acknowledge difficulty honestly** — stakeholders respect honesty
4. **Connect to strategy** — why does this matter in context of our plan?
5. **Call to action** — what do you want the audience to do?

### Crisis Communication Rules
1. Acknowledge — "We are aware and taking this seriously"
2. Fact-only — no speculation, no estimates you can't defend
3. Action — what are you doing about it?
4. Update commitment — "We will provide an update by [time]"
5. Never: "no comment," minimize, blame, or speculate

### Internal Announcement Structure
```
Subject: [Clear, specific subject]
From: [Executive name and title]
To: [Audience — All Company | Department | Leadership]

[OPENING: The news in one sentence]

[CONTEXT: Why this decision was made]

[WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU: Specific impact on audience]

[WHAT HAPPENS NEXT: Timeline and next steps]

[CLOSING: Reaffirm commitment, provide contact for questions]
```

## Tone by Audience
| Audience | Tone |
|---|---|
| Board | Direct, data-rich, options with recommendation |
| Investors | Confident, evidence-based, forward-looking with appropriate caution |
| Employees | Honest, empathetic, clear, action-oriented |
| Media | Factual, concise, on-message |
| Customers | Focused on their experience, solution-oriented |
| Regulators | Formal, precise, complete |
