# Skill: Crisis Management

## Purpose
Patterns for managing enterprise crises — from classification through resolution and post-crisis review.

## When to Invoke
- Active crisis or incident response
- Crisis preparedness planning
- Post-crisis review

## Crisis Classification Speed Guide

Answer 3 questions in the first 10 minutes:

1. **Is there immediate harm to people?** → If YES: L4 immediately
2. **Is there regulatory reporting obligation?** → If YES: GC stop-the-line, L3 minimum
3. **Will this appear in media within 24 hours?** → If YES: L3 minimum, Corp Comms activated

## First 60 Minutes — Crisis Checklist

```
MINUTE 0–10: CLASSIFY
  [ ] Incident described in 2 sentences
  [ ] Level classified (L1–L4)
  [ ] Incident Commander identified
  [ ] Crisis team convened (per level)

MINUTE 10–30: CONTAIN
  [ ] Immediate harm stopped or contained (if applicable)
  [ ] Evidence preserved (do NOT delete anything)
  [ ] Regulatory clock started (if applicable)
  [ ] Key facts confirmed (separate from speculation)

MINUTE 30–60: COMMUNICATE
  [ ] Internal notification sent to command team
  [ ] Board notified (L3/L4 only)
  [ ] Holding statement drafted (if media inquiry expected)
  [ ] Regulatory notification initiated (if required)
  [ ] Single spokesperson designated
```

## Holding Statement Templates

### Data Breach
"We are aware of a security incident that may have affected [customer | employee] data. We take the security of [audience]'s information extremely seriously. We have engaged [external forensics firm] and are working urgently to understand the full scope of the incident. We will provide an update as soon as we have confirmed facts. [Company] is committed to full transparency and will notify affected individuals as required by law."

### Regulatory Investigation
"[Company] has received an inquiry from [Regulator]. We are cooperating fully and taking this matter seriously. As this is an ongoing matter, we are unable to comment further at this time."

### Leadership Crisis
"[Company] has become aware of [issue]. We take [issue type] extremely seriously and have [initiated investigation | taken action]. We are committed to [value statement]. [CEO/Board] is personally overseeing our response."

## Post-Crisis Review (30 Days)

Required within 30 days of resolution:
1. Timeline reconstruction (what happened, minute by minute)
2. Root cause analysis (5 whys or similar)
3. Response effectiveness review (what worked, what didn't)
4. Control gaps identified
5. Corrective action plan (specific, owned, dated)
6. Process improvements to prevent recurrence
7. Board/Audit Committee briefing
