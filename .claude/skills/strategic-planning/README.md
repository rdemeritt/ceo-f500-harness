# Skill: Strategic Planning

## Purpose
Frameworks and patterns for developing, communicating, and executing corporate strategy.

## When to Invoke
- Annual strategic planning cycle
- Mid-year strategy review
- New market entry analysis
- Product or business model pivots
- Competitive response planning

## Core Frameworks

### Where to Play / How to Win
The essential strategic choice framework:
1. **What is our winning aspiration?** (Not "be the best" — what specific winning looks like)
2. **Where will we play?** (Markets, geographies, segments, channels — and explicit NOTs)
3. **How will we win?** (Differentiation — what we offer that others can't or won't)
4. **What capabilities must we have?** (To win in chosen markets)
5. **What management systems support this?** (Measurement, governance, incentives)

### OKR Structure
```
Objective: [Inspiring, directional, qualitative]
  Key Result 1: [Metric] from [baseline] to [target] by [date]
  Key Result 2: [Metric] from [baseline] to [target] by [date]
  Key Result 3: [Metric] from [baseline] to [target] by [date]
```
Rules: 3–5 OKRs per level; KRs must be measurable; stretch (70% achievement = success).

### Resource Allocation Discipline
- Rank all initiatives by strategic priority × financial return
- Fund top tier fully; underfund nothing (kills ROI)
- Explicitly cut bottom tier — scarcity creates focus
- Reserve 10–15% for opportunistic bets

## Communication Patterns
- Board: "Where to play, how to win, why we'll succeed"
- Organization: "Here's what we're doing, why it matters to you, what you need to do"
- Investors: "Our strategy, our differentiation, our financial model, our milestones"
