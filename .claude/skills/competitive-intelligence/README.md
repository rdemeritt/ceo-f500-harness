# Skill: Competitive Intelligence

## Purpose
Patterns for gathering, analyzing, and acting on competitive intelligence ethically and legally.

## When to Invoke
- Competitive analysis for strategic planning
- Win/loss analysis
- Market positioning decisions
- M&A target landscape review

## Legal Boundaries — Always Active

GC review required before:
- Any intelligence-gathering that involves accessing non-public competitor data
- Any reverse engineering of competitor products
- Any hiring of competitor employees with intent to extract proprietary information
- Any misrepresentation to gather information (illegal in most jurisdictions)

What is legal:
- Public filings (SEC, regulatory)
- Published press releases, marketing materials, job postings
- Customer and prospect interviews about competitive experience
- Industry analyst reports
- Product purchases and legitimate reverse engineering for interoperability
- Trade show and conference observations

## Competitor Analysis Framework

### One-Page Competitor Profile
```
COMPETITOR PROFILE — [Company Name]
Updated: [ISO date]

BASICS:
  Founded:          [Year]
  HQ:               [Location]
  Ownership:        [Public/Private/PE-backed]
  Revenue (est.):   $[X] ([Source])
  Employees (est.): [X] ([Source])
  Funding:          $[X] ([Stage, if private])

PRODUCTS/SERVICES:
  Core offering:    [Description]
  Target customer:  [ICP]
  Pricing model:    [How they charge]
  Pricing level:    [Premium/Mid/Value]

GO-TO-MARKET:
  Primary channel:  [Direct/Channel/Product-led]
  Marketing:        [Key messages and channels]
  Sales motion:     [Enterprise/SMB/PLG]

STRENGTHS (vs. us):
  1. [Specific advantage]
  2. [Specific advantage]

WEAKNESSES (vs. us):
  1. [Specific weakness]
  2. [Specific weakness]

RECENT MOVES:
  [Last 3 significant moves: product launches, hires, funding, partnerships]

WIN/LOSS vs. THIS COMPETITOR (last quarter):
  Wins:    [X] out of [Y] competitive deals
  Losses:  [X] out of [Y] — top reasons: [list]
```

## Win/Loss Analysis Pattern

Conduct within 48 hours of competitive deal close:
- Why did we win/lose? (Customer perspective, not sales team opinion)
- What was the deciding factor?
- What did competitor do better/worse?
- What would have changed the outcome?
- Update competitive playbook based on patterns

## Competitive Response Protocol

When a competitor makes a significant move (product launch, price change, major win):
1. CMO + CRO assess impact within 48 hours
2. CEO briefed within 72 hours
3. Strategic response options developed (match, counter, ignore)
4. CEO decides — document rationale
5. Sales team briefed with updated competitive battlecard
