# Skill: Regulatory Compliance

## Purpose
Patterns for maintaining regulatory compliance across applicable frameworks, managing filing calendars, and responding to regulatory inquiries.

## When to Invoke
- Identifying regulatory obligations for a new initiative
- Preparing regulatory filings
- Responding to regulator inquiries
- Conducting compliance reviews

## Compliance Calendar Management

Maintain a rolling 12-month compliance calendar:

```
COMPLIANCE FILING CALENDAR
Updated: [ISO date]
Owner: General Counsel

QUARTERLY FILINGS (Public Companies):
  10-Q Filing:       Due [45 days after quarter-end]
  SOX 302 Cert:      Due with 10-Q
  State tax filings: [per state — list each]

ANNUAL FILINGS (Public Companies):
  10-K Filing:       Due [60 days after fiscal year-end for large accelerated filers]
  SOX 404 Report:    Due with 10-K
  Proxy Statement:   Due [40 days before annual meeting]
  Annual Meeting:    [Date]

DATA PRIVACY:
  GDPR Records of Processing: Maintain continuously; review annually
  CCPA Annual Privacy Notice: January 1 each year
  Breach Notification:        72 hours (GDPR) / 30-72 days (US states, varies)

EMPLOYMENT:
  EEO-1 Report:      September 30 annually
  OSHA 300 Log:      February 1 – April 30 each year
  VETS-4212:         September 30 annually (if federal contractor)

INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC:
  [Add per industry — GC maintains]
```

## Regulatory Inquiry Response Protocol

When a regulatory inquiry is received:
1. **Day 0** — GC notified immediately; preserve all relevant documents (litigation hold)
2. **Day 1** — GC assesses nature and severity; engage outside regulatory counsel if warranted
3. **Day 2** — CEO and Board Audit Committee notified if material
4. **Day 3** — Initial response strategy determined
5. **Ongoing** — All regulator communications through GC only; no business team direct contact

## Never Do
- Never ignore a regulatory inquiry — silence implies admission
- Never destroy documents after inquiry received (spoliation)
- Never let business teams communicate directly with regulators
- Never minimize or withhold material information from regulators

## Common Regulatory Frameworks Quick Reference

| Framework | Applies To | Key Obligation |
|---|---|---|
| SOX | US public companies | Financial controls + CEO/CFO certification |
| SEC Reg | US public + reporting companies | Disclosure obligations |
| GDPR | Companies processing EU data | Consent, rights, breach notification |
| CCPA/CPRA | California-connected businesses | Notice, opt-out, data rights |
| FCPA | US companies with international operations | Anti-bribery |
| WARN Act | US employers ≥100 employees | 60-day layoff notice |
| ADA/EEOC | US employers | Employment discrimination |
| HIPAA | Healthcare data handlers | Privacy and security of PHI |
