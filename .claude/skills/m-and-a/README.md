# Skill: M&A

## Purpose
Patterns for deal evaluation, due diligence execution, valuation, and integration planning.

## When to Invoke
- Evaluating an acquisition target
- Conducting or coordinating due diligence
- Developing a deal thesis
- Planning post-merger integration

## Deal Thesis Framework
Every deal must have a clear thesis before diligence begins:

```
DEAL THESIS — [Target Name]
Date: [ISO date]

STRATEGIC RATIONALE:
  Type: [Market expansion | Product extension | Talent acquisition |
         Technology acquisition | Vertical integration | Consolidation]
  Thesis: [Why this target + why now, in 3 sentences]

FINANCIAL CASE:
  Entry valuation: $[X] ([X]x revenue / [X]x EBITDA)
  Comparable transactions: [3 comps with multiples]
  Synergy estimate: $[X] ([revenue synergies $X + cost synergies $X])
  Synergy timeline: Year 1: $X | Year 2: $X | Year 3: $X
  Pro forma IRR: [%]
  Payback period: [years]

RISKS:
  Top 3 deal risks and mitigation thesis:
  1. [Risk] — [Mitigation]
  2. [Risk] — [Mitigation]
  3. [Risk] — [Mitigation]

WALK-AWAY CRITERIA:
  We will not proceed if:
  - Revenue quality below [threshold]
  - IP ownership issues found
  - Regulatory clearance unlikely
  - Price exceeds $[X]
```

## Valuation Approaches
1. **Comparable companies** — EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA multiples of public comps
2. **Comparable transactions** — Deal multiples from recent M&A in the space
3. **DCF** — Free cash flow model with terminal value
4. **LBO analysis** — What can a financial buyer pay? (sets floor)

Use all four. Triangulate to a range. Present range to Board, not a single number.

## Integration Planning Essentials
Start planning Day 1 operating model before signing:
- Which systems will survive?
- Which team is the "keeper" in each function?
- What's the communication plan for customers, employees, vendors?
- What's the revenue protection plan for first 90 days?
- Who owns integration? (Dedicated integration leader, not the CEO)
